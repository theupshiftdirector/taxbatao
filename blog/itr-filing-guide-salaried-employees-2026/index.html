ITR Filing Guide for Salaried Employees 2026 - Step-by-Step

Master ITR filing for salaried employees in 2026 with tax slabs, deductions, and step-by-step instructions.

Understanding ITR Filing for Salaried Employees in 2026

Income Tax Return (ITR) filing is a mandatory requirement for salaried employees in India earning above the basic exemption limit. For the Financial Year 2025-26 (Assessment Year 2026-27), the government has set specific thresholds and tax slabs that every employee must understand.

As a salaried employee, you earn income primarily from your employer in the form of salary, which includes basic pay, allowances, bonuses, and perquisites. Filing your ITR not only ensures you comply with the Income Tax Act, 1961, but also helps you claim refunds if tax has been over-deducted from your salary.

The deadline for ITR filing for FY 2025-26 is 31st July 2026 for most taxpayers, and 31st October 2026 if you are a specified person (such as a director or partner in a firm).

Tax Slabs for FY 2025-26 (AY 2026-27)

India operates two tax regimes: the Old Regime and the New Regime. Every salaried employee must choose which regime to file under. Here are the applicable tax slabs for FY 2025-26:

The New Regime is the default option from FY 2023-24 onwards. However, you can choose the Old Regime if you have significant deductions available under sections 80C, 80D, 80CCD, etc. Most salaried employees benefit from the New Regime due to lower tax rates and fewer compliance requirements.

Old Regime vs New Regime: Which Should You Choose?

Old Regime Benefits: If you have substantial deductions through life insurance premiums (Section 80C - up to ₹1.5 lakhs), health insurance (Section 80D - up to ₹25,000 for self, ₹50,000 if you cover parents), or NPS contributions (Section 80CCD - up to 10% of salary), the Old Regime may result in lower tax liability.

New Regime Benefits: Offers lower tax rates and no Section 80 deductions required. Simpler to file, with no need to maintain documents for deductions. The standard deduction under the New Regime is ₹50,000 for salaried employees.

Example: If you earn ₹15,00,000 per year with ₹4,00,000 in deductions (life insurance + NPS), your taxable income under Old Regime would be ₹11,00,000 (tax: ₹1,80,000). Under New Regime, taxable income is ₹14,50,000 (tax: ₹2,17,500). Here, Old Regime saves ₹37,500.

Standard Deductions and Allowances Under Old Regime

When filing under the Old Regime, you can claim several deductions that reduce your taxable income. Here are the key sections utilized by salaried employees:

  • Section 80C (₹1.5 lakhs): Life Insurance Premiums, EPF contributions, ELSS mutual fund investments, principal repayment on home loans
  • Section 80D (₹25,000 to ₹50,000): Health insurance premiums for self and dependents
  • Section 80CCD (₹50,000 + 10% of salary): NPS (National Pension Scheme) contributions
  • Section 24(b) (₹2 lakhs): Home loan interest on self-occupied property
  • Section 80E: Student loan interest (no limit)
  • Standard Deduction (₹50,000): Available to all salaried employees in both regimes

Important: Under the New Regime, you cannot claim Section 80 deductions, but you receive a Standard Deduction of ₹50,000 automatically.

Step-by-Step ITR Filing Process for Salaried Employees

Filing your ITR involves several key steps. Most salaried employees file using ITR-1 (Sahaj) or ITR-2 forms:

  1. Gather Documents: Collect your Form 16 from employer, bank statements, investment receipts (insurance premiums, NPS statements), home loan documents, health insurance bills
  2. Choose Tax Regime: Decide between Old and New Regime based on your deductions. Run calculations using our tax calculator to compare both options
  3. Register on e-Filing Portal: Visit incometaxindiaefiling.gov.in and create an account using your PAN and Aadhaar
  4. Download Your Form 16: Your employer provides Form 16 showing salary paid and tax deducted (TDS)
  5. Fill ITR Form: Use the online form to input income, deductions, and tax details. ITR-1 is for individuals with only salary income
  6. Validate and Submit: The system validates your return for errors. Once submitted, you get an acknowledgment
  7. File Digitally or Upload Signed PDF: For returns over ₹50 lakh, digital signature verification or physical submission is required. Most salaried employees with lower incomes can e-verify through OTP

The entire process takes 30-60 minutes once you have all documents ready.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the filing deadline for ITR FY 2025-26?

The deadline for ITR filing for Financial Year 2025-26 (Assessment Year 2026-27) is 31st July 2026 for regular taxpayers. However, if you are required to get your accounts audited or are a specified person (director, partner, etc.), the deadline extends to 31st October 2026.

Do I need to file ITR if my income is below the exemption limit?

If your total income is below ₹3,00,000 (basic exemption limit for FY 2025-26), you are generally not required to file ITR. However, filing is beneficial if tax has been deducted from your salary—you can claim a refund. Additionally, filing helps for future loan applications and maintains a clean tax record.

What is Form 16 and why do I need it for ITR filing?

Form 16 is a certificate issued by your employer showing your total salary paid and tax deducted at source (TDS). It is essential for ITR filing as it documents your income and TDS payment. Without Form 16, you cannot accurately file your ITR. Your employer must issue it by 31st May 2026.

Can I switch from Old Regime to New Regime every year?

Yes, you can switch between Old and New Regime every financial year. However, once you opt for the New Regime, you cannot claim any Section 80 deductions for that year. Evaluate both options annually using our calculator to determine which regime benefits you most.

What happens if I file my ITR late?

Filing ITR after the deadline attracts penalties under Section 139(1): a penalty of ₹5,000 to ₹10,000 depending on your total income. However, your ITR will still be accepted if filed before 31st December 2026. Late filing may also delay refund processing and affect future loan approvals.

How do I claim HRA exemption in my ITR?

To claim HRA exemption under Section 10(13A), you must live in rented accommodation and receive HRA from your employer. The exemption is the lowest of: (1) HRA received, (2) 50% of salary in metro cities or 40% in other cities, or (3) Actual rent paid minus 10% of salary. Attach rent receipts and tenancy agreement with your ITR.
