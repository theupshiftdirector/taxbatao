Important Tax Deductions for FY 2025-26

Under the old tax regime, several deductions can significantly reduce your taxable income:

  • Section 80C (₹1.5 lakhs max): Includes investments in PPF, ELSS, life insurance premiums, home loan principal, tuition fees, and sukanya samriddhi scheme
  • Section 80D (₹25,000 to ₹1 lakh): Health insurance premiums for self and dependents. Senior citizens can claim up to ₹1 lakh
  • Section 80E: Interest on education loans (no limit) for you and your children
  • Section 80CCD(1) (₹1.5 lakhs): Employee contribution to National Pension System (NPS). Combined limit with 80C is ₹1.5 lakhs
  • Section 80CCD(1B) (₹50,000): Additional NPS contribution beyond the 80C limit
  • Section 80G: Donations to charitable organizations (50% or 100% of income based on type)
  • Section 80TTA: Interest on savings accounts (₹10,000 max) for non-senior citizens
  • Section 80TTB: Interest on deposits (₹50,000 max) for senior citizens

These deductions reduce your taxable income, not your tax liability directly. For instance, if you earn ₹15 lakhs and claim ₹3 lakhs in deductions, you pay tax on ₹12 lakhs only.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the income tax rate for FY 2025-26 in India?

For AY 2026-27 (FY 2025-26), tax rates under the new regime range from 5% on income above ₹3 lakh to 30% on income above ₹15 lakhs. Senior citizens (60-80 years) enjoy lower rates starting from 10%, while super seniors (above 80) get exemption up to ₹5 lakhs. Additionally, a 4% health and education cess applies to all taxpayers, and a 10% surcharge applies if total income exceeds ₹50 lakhs.

Should I choose the old tax regime or new tax regime for 2025-26?

Choose the old regime if you have significant deductions (₹80,000+) under sections 80C, 80D, and others. Choose the new regime if you have minimal deductions and want simpler taxation. The best way is to calculate tax under both regimes using our income tax calculator and compare which gives lower liability. Most salaried employees with good investment discipline benefit from the old regime.

Can I claim Section 80C deduction in both old and new tax regimes?

No. Section 80C deductions (₹1.5 lakh limit) are <strong>only available under the old tax regime</strong>. The new tax regime does not allow any deductions, though it offers lower tax rates. You must choose one regime and cannot claim 80C deductions if you opt for the new regime.

What is the maximum deduction under Section 80D for health insurance in 2025-26?

For individuals below 60 years, the maximum deduction under Section 80D is ₹25,000 per financial year. For senior citizens (60-80 years), the limit is ₹50,000. Super seniors (above 80 years) can claim up to ₹1 lakh for health insurance premiums on themselves and dependents.

Is my income taxable if I earn below ₹3 lakhs in FY 2025-26?

No income tax is applicable if your total income is up to ₹3 lakhs for individuals below 60 years under both tax regimes. For senior citizens (60-80), the exemption is ₹5 lakhs, and for super seniors (above 80), it's ₹5 lakhs. However, you may still need to file an ITR for other compliances.
