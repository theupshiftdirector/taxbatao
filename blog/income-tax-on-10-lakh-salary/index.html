Analysis: ₹10 Lakh Salary

A ₹10 lakh annual salary places you firmly in India's middle-income bracket — typically a mid-level professional, manager, or specialist in a metropolitan area. For a ₹10 lakh salary with only the standard deduction, the New Regime saves ₹1,06,600 in tax (10.7% of gross salary). However, if you have significant deductions under 80C, 80D, HRA, or home loan interest, the old regime may be more beneficial.

The New Regime is better by ₹1,06,600 assuming only the standard deduction. To make the Old Regime worth choosing, you would need additional deductions (beyond the standard ₹50,000) totalling approximately ₹5,33,000 — for example, ₹1.5L under 80C + ₹25,000 under 80D + HRA or home loan interest making up the balance. How to Save Tax on ₹10 Lakh Salary

Under the Old Regime, deductions can significantly reduce your tax liability. Here are the most impactful tax-saving options:

  • Section 80C (₹1.5 lakh limit): Invest in PPF, ELSS mutual funds, NSC, 5-year FD, or pay life insurance premium. This is the most widely-used deduction and reduces your taxable income by up to ₹1.5 lakh under the old regime.
  • Section 80D (health insurance): Premium paid for self/family health insurance is deductible up to ₹25,000 (₹50,000 if 60+ years). This is a zero-effort deduction available in the old regime.
  • NPS contribution (₹50,000 extra under 80CCD(1B)): Over and above the ₹1.5L 80C limit, contributing ₹50,000/year to NPS gives an additional deduction — saving ₹10,400 in tax at your income level.
  • HRA exemption (if renting): If you live in a rented house and receive HRA from your employer, the exemption can be substantial — especially in metros. Ensure you submit rent receipts and the landlord's PAN (if rent > ₹1L/year).
  • Switch regime annually if beneficial: You can switch between Old and New Regime every year (salaried employees can change at filing time). Compare both regimes with your actual deductions using Tax Batao before filing.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much income tax on ₹10 lakh salary under New Regime FY 2025-26?

Under the New Tax Regime for FY 2025-26, income tax on ₹10 lakh salary is ₹0 (including 4% cess). Taxable income after ₹75,000 standard deduction is ₹9,25,000. Monthly take-home: ₹83,333.

How much income tax on ₹10 lakh salary under Old Regime FY 2025-26?

Under the Old Tax Regime, tax on ₹10 lakh salary (with only standard deduction of ₹50,000) is ₹1,06,600. With full 80C (₹1.5L), 80D (₹25,000), and other deductions, this can be significantly lower. Use the Tax Batao calculator to compare with your actual deductions.

Which tax regime is better for ₹10 lakh salary?

With standard deduction only, the New Regime saves ₹1,06,600 in tax for a ₹10 lakh salary. However, this changes based on your actual deductions. Taxpayers with high 80C, 80D, HRA, or home loan interest should compare both regimes with their real numbers.

What is the monthly take-home for ₹10 lakh annual salary?

Monthly take-home for ₹10 lakh annual salary: ₹83,333/month under New Regime, ₹74,450/month under Old Regime. Note: actual take-home also depends on PF contributions, professional tax, and other payroll deductions by your employer.

Can I switch from Old to New Regime or vice versa?

Yes — salaried employees can switch between regimes every year at the time of filing their ITR. The default regime is New Regime; you need to opt-in for Old Regime explicitly. It is advisable to compare both regimes each year as your income and deductions change.
