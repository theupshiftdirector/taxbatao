Analysis: New Regime is Better

For a gross salary of ₹30 Lakh with only standard deduction, the new regime saves ₹2,49,600 compared to the old regime. The new regime benefits from wider tax slabs and a higher standard deduction of ₹75,000.

The effective tax rate under the best regime is 15.9%, meaning your annual take-home (before other deductions) would be approximately ₹25,24,200 or ₹2,10,350/month.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much tax on ₹30 Lakh salary under new regime?

Under the new tax regime for FY 2025-26, the total income tax on a salary of Rs 30 Lakh is Rs 4,75,800 (including 4% cess). The taxable income after Rs 75,000 standard deduction is Rs 29,25,000.

How much tax on ₹30 Lakh salary under old regime?

Under the old tax regime for FY 2025-26, the income tax on Rs 30 Lakh salary is Rs 7,25,400 (with only standard deduction of Rs 50,000, no other deductions). With additional deductions like 80C, 80D, and HRA, the tax can be significantly lower.

Which regime is better for ₹30 Lakh salary?

For a salary of Rs 30 Lakh with only standard deduction, the New Regime is better, saving Rs 2,49,600. However, if you have significant deductions (80C, 80D, HRA, home loan), the old regime might save more. Use our calculator to compare with your actual deductions.

What is the effective tax rate on ₹30 Lakh?

The effective tax rate on Rs 30 Lakh salary is 15.9% under the best regime. This means for every Rs 100 earned, you pay Rs 15.9 as income tax.
